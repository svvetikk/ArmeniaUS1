St. Mashtots Cathedral

Can you imagine speaking English but having to read and write in it using Greek letters? Pretty tough! Almost impossible!
That's how Armenians communicated for many centuries until the great scholar Mesrop Mashtots created the Armenian alphabet in 405 A.D.
This cathedral, in the village of Oshakan, is named after and is the final resting place of St. Mesrop Mashtots, the founder of the Armenian alphabet. The church you see here was built in 1875 on the grounds of a much older chapel that stood here from
